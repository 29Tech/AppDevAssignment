The National Hydrogen Strategy is Singapore's plan to explore and develop hydrogen as a key clean energy source to support its transition to a low-carbon future. Recognizing hydrogen's potential for decarbonizing various sectors, such as power generation, transportation, and industry, the strategy focuses on advancing research, pilot projects, and international collaborations to build hydrogen supply chains and infrastructure. By investing in hydrogen technologies and fostering partnerships, Singapore aims to diversify its energy mix, reduce carbon emissions, and enhance energy security, aligning with its long-term goal of achieving net-zero emissions by mid-century.
