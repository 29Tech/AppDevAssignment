The Zero Waste Masterplan is Singapore's comprehensive strategy to achieve a sustainable, waste-free future by reducing waste generation, increasing recycling rates, and minimizing landfill dependency. The plan focuses on key areas such as extending the lifespan of Semakau Landfill, promoting circular economy practices, and implementing mandatory packaging reporting and food waste reduction initiatives. It also emphasizes public education and industry collaboration to encourage responsible consumption and waste management. By targeting a 30% reduction in waste sent to landfills by 2030 and aiming for a 70% overall recycling rate, the Zero Waste Masterplan aligns with Singapore's broader sustainability goals under the Singapore Green Plan 2030.
