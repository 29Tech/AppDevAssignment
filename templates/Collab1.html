The Singapore Green Plan 2030 is a national sustainability agenda aimed at combating climate change and securing a greener future for Singapore. It focuses on five key pillars: City in Nature, which enhances green spaces and biodiversity; Sustainable Living, promoting eco-friendly practices among individuals and communities; Energy Reset, transitioning to cleaner energy and boosting energy efficiency; Green Economy, fostering green industries and innovation; and Resilient Future, building climate resilience and food security. Key targets include reducing emissions, increasing solar energy deployment, planting 1 million trees, and achieving net-zero emissions as soon as viable. The plan involves collaboration across government, businesses, and communities, positioning Singapore as a global leader in sustainability while ensuring a livable and resilient city for future generations.
