The OneMillionTrees movement is a nationwide initiative in Singapore aimed at planting one million trees across the island over a decade, as part of the Singapore Green Plan 2030. Led by the National Parks Board (NParks), the movement seeks to enhance urban greenery, restore natural ecosystems, and improve biodiversity while mitigating the effects of climate change. By involving communities, organizations, and volunteers in tree-planting activities, the initiative also fosters a sense of environmental stewardship and connection to nature. The movement contributes to creating a cooler, greener, and more sustainable living environment for future generations.
