The HDB Green Towns Programme is an initiative by Singapore's Housing and Development Board (HDB) aimed at making public housing estates more sustainable and environmentally friendly. Launched as part of the Singapore Green Plan 2030, the program focuses on reducing energy consumption, enhancing green spaces, and promoting water conservation across HDB towns. Key efforts include installing solar panels to harness renewable energy, implementing smart lighting and energy-efficient systems, increasing urban greenery through tree planting and rooftop gardens, and improving water management with features like rainwater harvesting. The program aims to create cooler, greener, and more sustainable living environments for residents while contributing to Singapore's broader climate goals.
